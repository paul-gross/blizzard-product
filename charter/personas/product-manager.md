# `persona:product-manager` — the product manager

## Who they are

They own *what gets built and in what order*. They are fluent in the backlog and the roadmap, not in harnesses or environments — and the product should never ask them to be. Their entire relationship with the fleet runs through its public surfaces: the work-source backlog (GitHub issues in the reference stack) and the hub's web app.

## What their work looks like

Their day is prioritization. Ideas and requests arrive, get shaped into issues, and get ordered; the fleet is simply the fastest pair of hands they have ever assigned work to. What changes with blizzard is not their craft but their reach: scheduling work for a fleet of agents should feel no heavier than assigning a ticket ever did.

## What they need from blizzard

- **Scheduling is one gesture.** Handing an issue's id to the hub is the entire act of putting work in front of the fleet. The issue itself stays in the backlog, where it already lives — blizzard never becomes a second place where work is defined.
- **The queue obeys them.** The ready queue's order — set at the hub, through the board's prioritize control — actually steers what the fleet pulls next. Priority is a decision they make, not a suggestion they submit.
- **The board answers "where is everything?"** without asking an engineer. Every item they scheduled is visible at its current station, and status on the board is status in fact.

## What serves them

The division of truth that keeps the backlog in the work source and execution state in the hub, the hub's PM wrapper, and the board.
